## One-Sentence Takeaway

Diffusion models learn generation by reversing a gradual corruption process, turning synthesis into a sequence of denoising decisions.

## Why It Matters

Diffusion models became central in image generation because they combine strong sample quality with a training objective that is often easier to stabilize than direct likelihood modeling.

## Core Ideas

### Forward Process

Noise is added progressively until data becomes close to simple random noise.

### Reverse Process

A model learns to predict denoising information so samples can be generated by stepping backward through the corruption chain.

### Guidance And Parameterization

Performance depends heavily on how the denoising target is parameterized and how conditioning or guidance is introduced at sampling time.

## Important Details

- Architecture: commonly a U-Net or transformer-based denoiser
- Objective: noise prediction, score matching, velocity prediction, or related variants
- Data: image, audio, video, or latent representations
- Evaluation: sample quality, diversity, controllability, and efficiency
- Strengths: high fidelity and flexible conditioning
- Limitations: slow sampling and sometimes opaque likelihood interpretation
